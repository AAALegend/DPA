Chapter 22

22 화

무연고자에게 신분을 만들어주는 건 다이분이 많이 해본 일이었다고 한다. 그는 황도 관리국，그러니까 말하자면 결국 근처 동사무소에 날 데려갔다. 담당자는 다이분을 하도 봤는지 또 왔냐는 듯 이골이 난 모 양이었다.

I was told that Daibun created a lot of identities for people that had no origins. He took me to the Imperial City Bureau, so to speak, to a nearby district office. The person in charge looked tried as if he had seen Daibun many times, maybe even questioning why he had come again.

“오늘은 웬일로 멀쩡하게 왔구만.”

“You’re looking quite neat today.”

“애 데려오는 건 처음이라서 말이지.”

“It’s because I’ve brought a child this time.”

“허，그런 거 신경 쓰는 줄은 몰랐네.”

“Hey, I didn’t know you actually cared that much.”

다이분은 오늘은 청소년 보호자 역 할이어서 그런지 오늘은 옷도 깨끗 하게 세탁된 걸 입었고 목욕도 한

Perhaps because Daibun played the role of my guardian today, he wore clean clothes and bathed today. I did the same. The person in charge didn’t seem to be all that fussy, as if this had already happened frequently except that we looked rather neat.

모양이었다. 그건 나도 마찬가지고. 깔끔하게 하고 온 것 말고는 자주 있던 일이라는 듯 담당자는 그리 까 탈스럽게 굴지 않았다.

별다른 의심도，별다른 절차도 그 리 많지 않았다.

He didn’t have any suspicions or questions, completing the procedure swiftly.

드레드핀드의 반지를 받았을 때 내 외형을 어떻게 설정할지 꽤나 고민했 었다. 일단 숱 많은 건 당연한 거고.

When I received the Dreadfiend’s ring, I thought long and hard as to what to change my appearance to. Naturally I should have a lot of hair.

I shouldn’t be too handsome or too ugly.

너무 잘생긴 것도 그렇고，그렇다 고 해서 너무 못생긴 것도 그렇고. 초미남으로 살아보고픈 생각은 있 지만 지나치게 외모로 어그로를 끌 생각은 없었다. 엘레리스가 애초에 그러지 말라고 말하기도 했고.

I did want to live as a super handsome man, but I didn’t want to attract too much attention with my looks. That was the first thing Eleris told me.

그래서 적당히 잘생긴 느낌으로. 머리는 금발에 약간 곱슬，눈동자는 파란색. 머리색도 눈동자 색깔도 그 리 드물지 않다. 평범한 것보다 좀 나은 외모로 충분했다. 쯤 생기긴 했는데 결국 그리 압도적 미남은 아 닌 외모다.

So I tried to make myself look moderately good-looking. Blonde hair, slightly curly, blue eyes. Neither the hair color nor the eye color were especially rare. It was good enough to looks slightly better than average. I was a bit good looking, but not overwhelmingly handsome.

“이름.”

“Name.”

“라인하르트입니다.”

“I’m Reinhardt.”

“출생지는?”

“Where were you born?”

“몰라요.”

“I don’t know.”

“후우…. 대충 어느 동네인지도 모 르겠어?”

“Whoa…. You don’t even know from what town you come from?”

담당자가 답답하다는 듯 날 바라봤다.

The person in charge looked at me rather frustrated.

“네. 어렸을 때 버려져서 기억이 안 나요.”

“Yes. I don’t really remember, because I was abandoned when I was very young.”

“…….”

이런 경우도 왕왕 있는 모양인지 그는 가타부타 별로 더 묻지 않았 다. 그는 테이블 앞에 있는 어떤 마 력석에 손을 갖다 대라고 했다. 그 는 뭔가를 확인하는 것 같더니 고개 를 끄덕였다.

He didn’t ask for much more information after that. He told me to put my hand on some mana stone in front of the table. He seemed to check something and then nodded.

“확실히 데이터가 없네…. 꽤 착하 게 살았나보구나.”

“There’s definitely no data on you… You must have lived a rather righteous life.”

뜬금없는 소리였지만 대강 이해는 된다. 저게 지문 인식기 같은 거라 면，내가 만약 자잘한 범죄들로 경비 대에 잡혀왔을 때 이미 내 데이터를 등록해뒀을 거다. 나 같은 무연고 애 들은 범죄를 저지를 가능성이 높은 데 그런 기록이 없는 걸 보고 착하 게 살았다 하는 것으로 추측된다.

He suddenly spoke some nonsense, but I could roughly understand what that meant. This seemed to be something like a fingerprint reader, so if I were caught by the guards after I committed even a petty crime, my data would’ve already been registered.  Homeless children like me were more likely to commit crimes, but seeing that I did not have such records, he presumed that I lived a righteous life.

결국 나는 출생지와 주소지가 불명 이지만，그걸 불명으로 둘 순 없었 기에 그 둘과 생년월일을 적당히 끼 워 맞췄다. 출생지는 옛날에 사라진 적당한 주소로，주소지는 브론즈게 이트 교각 남측으로.

After all, my birthplace and address were unknown, but I couldn’t just leave it blank, so I just made up a date of birth with those two. The birthplace was set as a suitable city that disappeared a long time ago and my address was south of the Bronzegate Bridge.

다리 하나를 소유한 것 같지만 결 국 다리 밑에 사는 거지란 얘기잖아 이거.

It seemed like I owned the bridge, but I actually lived under it.

마법도구가 발전한 편인 제국 특성 상，나는 사진까지 박힌 신분증을 얻을 수 있었다.

Due to the advanced nature of the empire, they had a magic tool with which I was able to get a photo for my ID card.

주기적으로 신분증을 갱신하라는 말과 함께 나와 다이분은 사무소를 나왔다.

Daibun and I left the office, sent off with the reminder to periodically come around to renew the ID.

그러니까.

So.

현대의 동사무소랑 하나도 다를 거 없다는 거다.

It really was no different from a modern district office.

지나치게 편의주의적이야! 약간 식 은땀이 날 정도로!

They were too expedient! To a shocking degree at that!

그래서 오히려 좋아!

However, that was good!

“꽤 간단한 편이네요.”

“That was pretty easy.”

“We gave him some things after all, so of course our stay would have been comfortable.”

“쥐여 준 게 있으니까 편해야지 당 연히.” 아무래도 저 담당자는 이런저런 향 응을 제공받는 것으로 추측된다. 지 나치게 프리패스처럼 느껴지긴 했단 말이지. 담당자 양반 저러는 거 들 키면 모가지가 분명해.

I assumed that the person in charge was provided with a variety of things. I did feel that he was too easy going. If he got caught doing things like this, the outcome would be pretty clear.

나는 금속 카드 형태로 된 신분증 을 확인했다.

I checked my ID that was in the form of a metal card.

사진과 이름，출생년도와 고유번호 가 적혀 있었다. 현대의 신분증이랑 크게 다를 게 없었다. 생체정보를 등록하는 것도 그렇고 말이지.

It showed a picture, my name, date of birth and identification number. It wasn’t much different from a modern ID card. The same went for the registration of biometric data.

무늬만 중세 판타지지 그냥 편의적 인 건 현대랑 크게 차이가 없다.

Only its appearance fit into medieval fantasy, but its convenience wasn’t much different from the modern world.

다시 말하지만 중세 판타지 특.

Once again, it was medieval Fantasy.

아무튼 그럼.

Anyway.

이 한마디로 정리됨.

To sum it up in a single sentence.

그래서 편하면 된 거다.

It was convenient.

아직도 거리는 승전의 여파로 들떠 있었다. 이 분위기는 아주 오랫동안 지속될 거다. 참전자들이 돌아오는 개선행사는 더더욱 엄청난 규모로 벌어질 테고 말이지.

The town was still in an excited frenzy as a result of their victory. That atmosphere would last for a very long time. The improvement event when the  war veterans returned would be on an even greater scale. 

긴 축제가 이어질 거다.

A long festival would follow.

신분이 생겼으니 템플에 입학하는 것도 술술 진행하기로 했다.

Now that I had an ID, I decided to smoothly proceed with my admission to Temple.

“그런데 템플이라는 게 돈만 있으 면 입학이 가능하긴 한 거냐? 평민 출신은 안 받아준다거나…. 그런 거 아니었어?”

“By the way, does Temple just admit anyone who has money? Wasn’t there something like… They won’t accept commoners or something?”

다이분은 의구심이 드는지 템플로 날 데려가는 길에도 고개를 갸웃거 렸다.

While we were on the way to Temple, Daibun tilted his head..

“일단 그렇다고는 하던데요.”

“Well, I never heard anything like that.”

학비가 어마어마하게 비싸서 귀족 자제들이 아니면 입학이 거의 불가 능한 거지. 대부호나 상인들의 자식 들도 왕왕 다닌다.

The tuition fees were just so high that it was almost impossible to enter the school unless one was a noble’s child, had wealthy parents or was part of a merchant’s family.

일 년에 억 단위의 학비를 내야 하고，전공이 무엇으로 정해지느냐 에 따라서，그리고 고등교육으로 넘 어갈수록 배 넘게 뛰는 학비를 부담 할 자신이 있다면 말이지만.

If one had the confidence to pay hundreds of millions of won a year, depending on what major one picked, one was free to go. If one wanted to pursue higher education one would have to expect to pay twice as much.

50골드라는 것도 결국 평균치일 뿐이다. 전공에 따라 말도 안 되게 학비가 상승하기도 한다.

물론 장학제도도 있고，제국 공훈 에 따라서 자녀들이 템플 학비를 면 제받는 경우도 있긴 하지만.

50 gold coins was the average. Depending on ones major, tuition fees could rise to unimaginable amounts. Of course, there was a scholarship system, and there were cases where children were exempted from the tuition depending on how much they contributed to the empire.

그래서 전쟁이 끝나고 엄청난 양의 훈장이 풀리면서 내년부터 템플은 평소보다 많은 신입생들로 붐비게 된다.

So, after the end of the war, a huge amount of medals would be handed out, leading the Temple to be crowded with more freshmen than usual starting with the next year.

템플은 일단 어마어마한 학비로 벌 어들인 돈을 재능 있는 아이들에게 재투자하기 위해 학비가 비싼 거다. 대부분은 그 금액에 해당하는 수준 의 교육을 받진 못한다 이거지.

Temple was so expensive because they reinvested that money earned from that huge tuition fee into talented children. Most don’t get that much education.

다이분은 날 데리고 에레디안 지구 로 향한다.

Daibun took me to the Eredian district.

서울로 치자면 관악구. 서울대 있 는 그곳.

That would be where the Gwanak district and Seoul National University were located in Korea.

다른 점이 있다면 템플은 관악구 일대 전체를 아우르는 초대규모 교 육기관이다. 그러니 학생 숫자가 어 마어마하게 많은 건 물론，벌어들이 는 돈이 천문학적인 것도 당연한 이 치다.

The only difference was that Temple was a much larger educational institution covering the entire Gwanak district. Therefore, it was only natural that the number of students was also astronomically high, meaning the money that went into this school was equally astronomical.

그뿐만이 아니다. 학생 이외의 사 람들이 나돌아다니는 것을 방지하기 위해，템플 지부 내에는 학생과 교 원，그리고 들어갈 수 있는 허가를 갖지 못한 이들의 진입을 차단하는 대규모 결계는 물론 물리적인 방벽 까지 쳐져있다. 내가 설정한 바로 템플의 경비와 방범 시스템，그리고 결계는 황성과 완전히 동일하다.

Not only that. In order to prevent non-students from wandering around the campus, Temple was equipped with physical barriers as well as large scale magic barriers that block those that weren’t students, faculty or people who had permission to enter. The Temple’s security, crime prevention system and barriers that I set up were exactly the same as the Imperial Castle’s.

각 국가에서 내로라하는 가문들 자 식이다. 그들이 안전한 상태에서 온 전히 수학에만 전념할 수 있는 환경 을 만들어놓은 것이다. 그렇기에 모 두가 안심하고 먼 길로 자녀들을 떠 나보내는 것이기도 하고. 어중이떠 중이들이 템플 부지에 돌아다니다가 학생들을 납치하거나 해코지를 당하 기라도 하면 큰일일 테니까 그만큼 안전대책이 중요한 법.

There were children said to come from each country’s powerful family, so they created an environment in which they could fully focus on their studies without worrying about their safety. That’s why everyone was able to send their children off to a far away place with a peace of mind and it would be a big deal if any of the students were to get harmed or abducted while they were walking around the campus, so they put huge emphasis on the safety measures.

Of course, there were loopholes, because an incident had to happen after all.

물론 사건이 발생해야 하니까 허점 이란 게 아예 없진 않지만. 방비와 대책이 지나치게 완벽하면 사건을 발생시킬 수 없다고. 그런 건 아무도 원하지 않아. 특히 내가 원하질 않지.

If the defense and countermeasures were too perfect, incidents wouldn’t be able to happen. No one wanted that. Especially me. 

아무튼 템플은 사실상 동네 이름이 나 다름없어서 템플에 도착했다는 건 애초에 좀 이치에 안 맞는 말이 다. 템플 내부로는 마력열차가 들어 갈 수도 없으니까.

Anyway, Temple was practically a town’s name, so it wouldn’t make a lot of sense to say we arrived at Temple to begin with. The mana train couldn’t even go inside of Temple.

우리가 도착한 건 템플 부지 바깥 에 있는 입학상담소였다. 다이분은 입을 다물지 못했다.

We arrived at the admission office outside Temple. Daibun couldn’t keep his mouth shut.

“이쪽은 처음 와보는데. 사람이 뭐 이렇게 많은 거냐?”

“This is my first time coming here, so why are there so many people here?”

입학상담소 자체만으로도 이미 하 나의 학교나 다름없을 정도로 거대 한데，더더욱 놀라운 건 그 입구 근 처에 수많은 사람들이 기다리고 있 었다.

The admission counseling center on its own was already huge enough to be called a school, and what was even more surprising was the number of people waiting near the entrance.

번호표를 받아들고 기다리는 사람 들이었고，전부 애들이랑 그 옆의 부모들이었다.

They were people waiting to get admitted. They all pulled a number ticket, all of whom were children with their parents next to them.

다이분은 세상에 애들 템플 보내려 는 부자들이 이렇게 많은 것이 그저 이해가 가지 않는 모양이었다.

Daibun just couldn’t understand that there were so many rich people out there trying to send their children to Temple.

이게 무슨 현상인지는 알고 있다.

I knew what this was about.

“아마 그런 게 아닐까요?”

“Maybe that’s what it is?”

“그런 거라니?”

“What do you mean?”

“특별한 재능을 가진 애들은 템플 에 공짜로 들어갈 수 있으니까. 그 걸 확인하려는 거겠죠.”

“Children with special talents can enter Temple for free. That’s what they’re trying to confirm here.”

“그런 경우도 있냐?”

“Will there even be cases like that?”

“사람 일은 모르는 거니까 말이죠.”

“One could never know.”

추측하듯 말했지만 사실이다. 템플 에서 교육받았다는 것만으로도 본인 의 가치가 확 뛰어오르는데，학비를 부담하긴 어렵지만 우리 애는 뭔가 특별한 힘이 있거나 천재일지도 모 른다고 믿는 부모들이 있다.

I put it as if it was just a guess, however it was actually the truth. Even though it was difficult for them to pay the tuition, there were some parents who thought their child had special powers or was a genius.

학비를 부담할만한 여력이 없는 부 모들은 혹시 모를 가능성에 기대 아 이를 데리고 템플에 오는 것이다.

Parents who couldn’t afford to pay the school fees would come here with their children, counting on that possibility.

물론 대부분 그런 건 없다는 사실 을 알고 쓸쓸히 돌아가지만. 분명히 재능을 발견하고 입학하는 아이들도 있다.

Of course, most of them get sent back disappointed that there was no talent to be found, but they definitely did discover children with special talents, allowing them to enter the school.

확률이 매우 낮을 수밖에 없다.

The probability was just very low.

이 세계에는 큰 틀 아래에서 네 가지 힘이 존재한다.

There were four forces in this world as a great whole.

전투，마법，신성력.

Combat, Magic, Divine power.

그리고 다른 세 힘과는 전혀 다른， 발견된 지 얼마 되지 않은 힘. 초능력.

And a power that was completely different from those three, a power that hadn’t been discovered even after a long time. Supernatural power.

전투는 그냥 몸 가지고 뛰어다니는 게 아니라 성장하면 초인에 가까운 힘을 발휘한다. 용사 아르토리우스 같은 경우가 그러했다. 날 추격하던 살레리언 공작가의 기사들 또한 그 런 힘을 갈고닦던 이들이었을 것이 다. 다만 마계 측에서도 최강의 괴 물들이 마왕성에 있었다. 가고일이 베테랑 기사를 일격에 작살낸 건 그 럴만한 이유가 있어서일 거다. 어쨌건，초인이 될 가능성이 있는 녀석들은 체술을 배우는 쪽으로 진 학할 것이다.

Combat wasn’t just about being able to run well, but being able to display superhuman powers after one grew up. Such was the case with Artorius. The Knights of Duke Salerian who pursued me must have been those with similar powers. However, even on the side of the Demon Realm, there were monsters a lot stronger. There was a good reason why the Gargoyle was able to crush these veteran knights in a single blow. 

In any case, those that have the potential to develop superhuman powers will go on to learn martial arts.

머리가 비정상적으로 똑똑하거나 마법관련 재능이 식별된 아이들은 마법 쪽 전공에 진학할 거다.

Children who were unusually smart or were discovered to have magic-related talents would go on to major in magic.

신성력에 재능을 보이는 이들은 신 관 혹은 성기사의 길을 가게 될 것 이다.

Those who show talent in divine power would go down the path of a priest or paladin.

그리고 극소수의 아이들은 때로 기 이한 능력을 각성한다. 초능력이라 명명된 이 힘은 아직 제대로 규명되 지 않았다. 마법처럼 배워서 사용하 는 힘이 아니기에 사람에 따라선 제 대로 통제되지 않는 경우도 있다.

And very rarely, children sometimes awakened to bizarre abilities. These powers, dubbed supernatural powers, had not yet been fully elucidated, because it wasn’t an ability that could be learned and used like magic. Some people might not even be able to control them properly.

그렇기에 제국은 이 초능력자들에 대해 각별하게 관리하고 있었다. 마 계대전에서 전력으로 사용하진 못했 지만 사람에 따라 극히 위험하기도 한 힘이었으니까. 정도에 따라 통제 가 불가능하다고 생각될 정도의 초 능력자들은 결국 은밀하게 처분되기 도 한다.

That was why the Empire was taking special care towards these supernatural powers. They weren’t used as resources in the Demon World War, but they could be very dangerous depending on the person. Depending on the level of danger, users with supernatural powers that were thought to be out of control would eventually be disposed of secretly.

초능력자들에게 템플은 그들이 능 력을 계발하는 동시에 통제하고 살 아갈 수 있도록 사회화시키는 기관 의 역할을 한다.

For children with supernatural powers, Temple served as an institution to socialize as well as learn how to control and develop their abilities.

물론 이 자리에 모인 대부분의 아 이들은 위의 네 경우 어디에도 해당 되지 않을 경우가 대부분일거다.

Of course, most of the children gathered here would most likely fall under none of these four classes.

재능은 흔하지 않다. 결코.

Having talent was rare. Really rare.

“그나저나 사람이 이렇게나 많은데 우리 차례가 오긴 하는 거냐?”

“By the way, with so many people here, would we even get our turn today?”

다이분은 좀 더 현실적인 문제를 고민하는 것 같았다. 확실히 저기 문 닫을 시간까지 기다려도 저기 들 어갈 수 있을지 어떨지 모를 지경이 긴 하지.

Daibun seemed to be thinking about a much more realistic problem. I was rather sure that if we were to line up there we would have to wait for a really long time. Perhaps so long until they closed their doors, so we couldn’t know if we would even be able to get in today.

물론 괜한 걱정이다.

Of course, that was an unnecessary worry.

“우린 입학 신청을 하러 온 거지 상담을 하러 온 게 아니잖아요?”

“We’re  here to apply for admission, not for consultation, right?”

우린 템플에 입학할만한 돈이 없어 서 무료입학을 상담 받으러 온 게 아니다.

We didn’t come here to try for the free admission, because we didn’t have enough money or something.

“아，그렇군.”

“Ah, that’s right.”

우리는 당당하게 입학 상담소가 아 니라 입학 신청을 위한 입구로 들어 갔다.

We proudly entered through the entrance to apply for admission, not into the admission counselation office.

입학 상담소 쪽과 달리 입학 신청 장소는 한산했다. 물론 사람들이 있 긴 했지만，그들이 대기하는 곳도 매우 고급스럽게 꾸며져 있었다.

Unlike the admission counselation office, the place to apply for admission was quiet. Of course, there were people there, but the place they waited in was decorated very luxuriously.

멀리서 온 사람들인지 아이랑 곧 생이별해야 된다는 생각에 훌쩍거리 는 부모들도，가기 싫다고 징징거리 는 아이들도 있었다.

There were some parents who bawled their eyes out at the thought that they had to say goodbye to their children soon, perhaps because they came from afar, and some children who whined that they didn’t want to go.

템플이 아무리 좋은 곳이라 해도 싫은 사람에겐 그냥 싫을 수 있는 거지. 애들이 뭘 알겠어. 부모랑 갑 자기 생이별한다는데.

No matter how good Temple was, you could always find people like that. What did they know? Just because they had to part with their parents.

“Ehm, this is the window for admission applications….”

“이곳은 입학 신청용 창구입니다 만….” 깔끔하긴 해도 전혀 돈 많아 보이 지 않는 행색의 나와 다이분을 보고 창구 직원이 부드럽게 다른 쪽을 안 내하려 했다.

Seeing me and Daibun, who looked rather neat but also rather penniless, the counter staff tried to gently guide us to the other side.

“어，어…. 어 그게….”

“U, uh…. That’s….”

다이분은 이런 어마어마한 부자들 이나 드나드는 곳에 와서인지 오히 려 제 쪽이 얼어있었다. 아니，보호 자가 졸면 어떡하는데?

Daibun froze by my side, perhaps because that was his first time coming to a place filled with enormously wealthy people. No, why would my guardian be scared, huh?

“돈 내고 입학하려고 온 건데요.”

“I came here to pay the admission fee.”

오히려 내 쪽이 태연하게 말하자 직원은 좀 당황하는 눈치였다.

On the contrary, when I spoke up calmly, the staff looked rather embarrassed.
